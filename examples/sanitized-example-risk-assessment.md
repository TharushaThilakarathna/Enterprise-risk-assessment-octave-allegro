# Sanitized Example Risk Assessment

## Overview

This example demonstrates how a cybersecurity risk assessment can be documented using OCTAVE Allegro principles.

The scenario below is fictional and contains no client information. It is included for educational and portfolio purposes only.

# Critical Asset Profile

## Asset Information

| Field          | Value                      |
| -------------- | -------------------------- |
| Asset Name     | Customer Management System |
| Asset Type     | Web Application            |
| Asset Owner    | Sales Department           |
| Classification | Confidential               |

---

## Business Importance

The Customer Management System stores customer records, contact information, sales activities, and account history. The application is critical for maintaining customer relationships and supporting daily business operations.

---

## Security Requirements

### Confidentiality

Only authorized employees should access customer information.

### Integrity

Customer records must not be modified without authorization.

### Availability

The system should be available during business hours with minimal downtime.

---

# Threat Scenario

## Threat Actor

External Cybercriminal

---

## Threat Description

An attacker attempts to gain unauthorized access to customer records using stolen employee credentials.

---

## Attack Method

* Credential theft
* Phishing attack
* Password reuse exploitation

---

## Vulnerability

Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

---

## Motivation

Financial gain through theft of customer information.

---

# Impact Assessment

| Impact Area         | Rating |
| ------------------- | ------ |
| Reputation          | High   |
| Financial           | Medium |
| Productivity        | Medium |
| Compliance          | High   |
| Customer Confidence | High   |

---

# Risk Analysis

## Likelihood

High

### Justification

Credential theft attacks are common and employees frequently use web-based services.

---

## Impact

High

### Justification

Unauthorized access could expose sensitive customer information and damage organizational reputation.

---

## Overall Risk Rating

### High

---

# Recommended Controls

## Administrative Controls

* Security awareness training
* Password management policy
* Regular access reviews

---

## Technical Controls

* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Security monitoring
* Account lockout policies
* Centralized logging

---

## Physical Controls

* Secure workstation access
* Visitor management controls

---

# Risk Treatment Decision

### Mitigate

The organization should implement technical and administrative controls to reduce the likelihood of unauthorized access.

---

# Residual Risk

After implementing MFA and monitoring controls, the likelihood of compromise is reduced; however, some residual risk remains due to evolving phishing techniques.

Residual Risk Rating:

### Medium

---

# Key Lessons Learned

* Identity security remains a critical control area.
* Multi-Factor Authentication significantly reduces account compromise risk.
* Security awareness training complements technical controls.
* Risk assessments should be reviewed periodically to address emerging threats.
