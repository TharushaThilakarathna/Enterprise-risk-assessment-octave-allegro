# Risk Treatment Strategies

## Overview

Risk treatment is the process of selecting and implementing actions that address identified risks.

The objective is to reduce risk exposure to an acceptable level while balancing operational, financial, and security requirements.

---

# Risk Treatment Process

```text
Identify Risk
      ↓
Evaluate Severity
      ↓
Select Treatment Option
      ↓
Implement Controls
      ↓
Monitor Effectiveness
      ↓
Review Residual Risk
```

---

# Risk Treatment Approaches

There are four primary risk treatment strategies.

---

## 1. Risk Mitigation

Risk mitigation reduces either the likelihood or impact of a threat.

Examples:

* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Security Awareness Training
* Encryption
* Access Control
* Vulnerability Management
* Network Segmentation

### Example

Risk:

Unauthorized access to sensitive information.

Mitigation:

* MFA
* Strong password policies
* Role-based access controls

---

## 2. Risk Acceptance

Organizations may choose to accept a risk when:

* Risk is low
* Treatment costs exceed benefits
* Risk falls within acceptable tolerance levels

Accepted risks should still be documented and monitored.

### Example

Risk:

Minor temporary service interruptions.

Decision:

Accept due to low business impact.

---

## 3. Risk Transfer

Risk transfer shifts responsibility to a third party.

Examples:

* Cyber insurance
* Managed Security Services
* Outsourced infrastructure providers

### Example

Risk:

Financial loss from data breaches.

Treatment:

Cybersecurity insurance coverage.

---

## 4. Risk Avoidance

Risk avoidance eliminates the activity causing the risk.

### Example

Risk:

Use of unsupported legacy software.

Treatment:

Retire the software and migrate to a supported platform.

---

# Residual Risk

Residual risk is the risk that remains after controls are implemented.

No control environment can completely eliminate risk.

Organizations must determine whether residual risk remains acceptable.

---

# Selecting Appropriate Controls

Effective controls should be:

* Cost-effective
* Practical
* Sustainable
* Aligned with business objectives
* Proportionate to the level of risk

---

# Control Categories

## Administrative Controls

Examples:

* Policies
* Procedures
* Security Awareness Training
* Risk Governance

---

## Technical Controls

Examples:

* Firewalls
* MFA
* Encryption
* Endpoint Protection
* Logging and Monitoring

---

## Physical Controls

Examples:

* CCTV
* Security Guards
* Access Badges
* Locked Server Rooms

---

# Monitoring and Review

Risk treatment is not a one-time activity.

Organizations should:

* Review controls regularly
* Monitor effectiveness
* Update risk assessments
* Track emerging threats
* Improve security practices continuously

---

# Conclusion

Effective risk treatment enables organizations to reduce exposure, improve resilience, and support long-term information security objectives through a structured and risk-based approach.
