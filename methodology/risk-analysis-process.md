# Risk Analysis Process

## Overview

Risk analysis is the process of identifying, evaluating, and prioritizing potential threats that could negatively impact an organization's information assets, business operations, and security objectives.

The purpose of risk analysis is to understand the likelihood and impact of adverse events and support informed decision-making regarding security investments and controls.

---

# Risk Analysis Lifecycle

```text
Identify Assets
       ↓
Identify Threats
       ↓
Identify Vulnerabilities
       ↓
Evaluate Likelihood
       ↓
Evaluate Impact
       ↓
Determine Risk Level
       ↓
Prioritize Risks
```

---

# Asset Identification

The first step is identifying assets that are important to the organization.

Examples include:

* Information assets
* Databases
* Business applications
* Servers
* Employee records
* Customer information
* Financial records

Assets should be prioritized according to business value and operational importance.

---

# Threat Identification

Threats are events or actors that may exploit vulnerabilities and cause harm.

Common threat sources include:

### External Threats

* Cybercriminals
* Malware
* Phishing attacks
* Ransomware
* Nation-state actors

### Internal Threats

* Human error
* Insider misuse
* Privilege abuse
* Accidental data exposure

---

# Vulnerability Identification

Vulnerabilities are weaknesses that may be exploited by threats.

Examples:

* Weak passwords
* Missing security patches
* Misconfigured systems
* Lack of monitoring
* Inadequate access controls

---

# Likelihood Assessment

Likelihood estimates how probable a threat scenario is.

Typical ratings:

| Level  | Description                  |
| ------ | ---------------------------- |
| High   | Expected to occur frequently |
| Medium | Could occur occasionally     |
| Low    | Unlikely but possible        |

---

# Impact Assessment

Impact measures the potential consequences if a threat occurs.

Common impact areas include:

* Reputation
* Financial Loss
* Productivity
* Legal Compliance
* Information Security

---

# Risk Calculation

Risk is commonly determined using:

```text
Risk = Likelihood × Impact
```

Higher likelihood and impact produce higher risk levels.

---

# Risk Prioritization

After calculating risks, they should be ranked to support decision making.

Typical priorities:

| Risk Level | Action                        |
| ---------- | ----------------------------- |
| Critical   | Immediate action required     |
| High       | Mitigate as soon as possible  |
| Medium     | Monitor and manage            |
| Low        | Accept or periodically review |

---

# Benefits of Risk Analysis

* Improved decision making
* Better resource allocation
* Enhanced security posture
* Reduced organizational exposure
* Stronger governance and compliance

---

# Conclusion

Risk analysis enables organizations to identify, understand, and prioritize cybersecurity risks, allowing management to implement effective controls and allocate resources where they are needed most.
