# OCTAVE Allegro Methodology Overview

## Introduction

Operationally Critical Threat, Asset, and Vulnerability Evaluation (OCTAVE) Allegro is an asset-centric information security risk assessment methodology developed by Carnegie Mellon University's Software Engineering Institute (SEI).

Unlike traditional risk assessment approaches that focus primarily on technical infrastructure, OCTAVE Allegro focuses on information assets and the business impact resulting from their compromise.

The methodology helps organizations identify critical information assets, evaluate potential threats, analyze risks, and determine appropriate mitigation strategies.

---

# Objectives

The primary objectives of OCTAVE Allegro are:

* Identify critical information assets
* Understand asset security requirements
* Analyze threat scenarios
* Evaluate business impact
* Prioritize organizational risks
* Support risk-based decision making
* Improve information security governance

---

# Core Principles

OCTAVE Allegro is based on the following principles:

### Asset-Centric Approach

The assessment focuses on information assets that are critical to business operations.

### Business Impact Driven

Risk evaluation is based on business impact rather than purely technical vulnerabilities.

### Organizational Context

Risks are evaluated within the context of the organization's operational environment.

### Structured Decision Making

The methodology provides a repeatable process for identifying and prioritizing risks.

---

# OCTAVE Allegro Process

The framework consists of eight major steps:

## 1. Establish Risk Measurement Criteria

Define how risks will be evaluated based on organizational priorities.

Common impact areas include:

* Reputation and Customer Confidence
* Financial Impact
* Productivity
* Legal and Regulatory Impact
* Safety and Health

---

## 2. Develop Information Asset Profiles

Identify critical information assets and document:

* Asset ownership
* Business value
* Security requirements
* Operational dependencies

---

## 3. Identify Information Asset Containers

Determine where information assets are stored, processed, or transmitted.

Examples:

* Databases
* Servers
* Cloud Platforms
* Physical Documents
* Personnel

---

## 4. Identify Areas of Concern

Identify situations that may negatively affect information assets.

Examples:

* Unauthorized access
* Data loss
* Insider threats
* Malware infections
* Service disruptions

---

## 5. Develop Threat Scenarios

Create realistic scenarios describing:

* Threat actors
* Attack methods
* Motivations
* Potential outcomes

---

## 6. Identify Risks

Evaluate how threat scenarios may affect confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

---

## 7. Analyze Risks

Assess:

* Likelihood of occurrence
* Business impact
* Risk severity
* Organizational exposure

Risk ratings are used to prioritize treatment efforts.

---

## 8. Select Mitigation Approaches

Determine appropriate risk treatment options.

Common strategies include:

### Mitigate

Implement controls to reduce risk.

### Accept

Formally acknowledge and tolerate the risk.

### Transfer

Transfer risk through insurance or third-party agreements.

### Defer

Delay treatment until resources become available.
